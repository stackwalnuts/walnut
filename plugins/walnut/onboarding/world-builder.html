walnut — world builder

Your world is about to come alive.

Walnut is a personal context infrastructure. Five domains. One system. Every project, person, and idea gets its own walnut — a living container that remembers everything across sessions, conversations, and tools.

What this unlocks:

  • • Persistent context — every session picks up exactly where you left off
  • • Cross-session memory — decisions, insights, and people tracked automatically
  • • Zero-context handoff — any agent can load your world and know everything
  • • Connected intelligence — your email, transcripts, and docs wired into context

Takes about 3 minutes. Or skip to the end with just a name and one walnut.

What are you building?

A walnut is a unit of context — anything with its own identity, lifecycle, and history. A startup. A book. A visa application. A health goal. Start with whatever's top of mind.

Each walnut gets its own log, tasks, insights, and session history. The system tracks everything independently — so context from one project never bleeds into another. You can always add more later with /walnut:create.

Your people

Who matters most right now? Co-founders, partners, clients, collaborators.

People get their own walnuts in your system. When someone is mentioned in a session, the system catches it and routes context to their file — preferences, updates, history. You build a living dossier on everyone who matters without trying.

Context sources

Where does your existing context live? The system won't import anything yet — it just knows what's available so it can search when relevant.

Common sources — click to add, or type your own below.

Context sources let the system search your existing knowledge when you need it. MCP sources are queried live. Exports are imported once. Vaults are searched as markdown. Nothing is imported until you say so.
